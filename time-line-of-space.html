Space Exploration Timeline

Konstantin Tsiolkovsky 1857-1935

Father of astronautic theory. Proposed rocket propulsion in space exploration.

"Earth is the cradle of humanity, but one cannot live in a cradle forever"

Robert Goddard 1882-1945

Launched first liquid-fueled rocket. Held 214 patents in rocketry.

Serene Tazkout Born 1985

Leading scientist in space technology and the first woman from the Arab world to hold a major leadership position at a European Space Agency mission.

"The space sector needs diversity to truly progress."

Ahmed Farouk Born 1980

Chief Engineer at the Egyptian Space Agency. Known for contributions to satellite development and the advancement of space exploration programs in Egypt.

Dr. Farouk El-Baz Born 1938

Geologist and space scientist. Worked with NASA on the Apollo lunar program. Contributed to selecting lunar landing sites for Apollo missions.

Yuri Gagarin April 12, 1961

Spacecraft: Vostok 1

Achievements:

  • First human in space and to orbit Earth.
  • Completed one orbit in 108 minutes.
  • Historic milestone in space exploration.

Alan Shepard May 5, 1961

Spacecraft: Mercury-Redstone 3

Achievements:

  • First American in space.
  • 15-minute suborbital flight.
  • Paved the way for future U.S. space missions.

John Glenn February 20, 1962

Spacecraft: Mercury-Atlas 6

Achievements:

  • First American to orbit Earth (3 orbits).
  • Mission duration: 4 hours, 55 minutes.
  • Key figure in advancing manned spaceflight.

Valentina Tereshkova June 16, 1963

Spacecraft: Vostok 6

Achievements:

  • First woman in space.
  • Spent 3 days in space, completing 48 orbits.
  • Conducted scientific experiments and Earth observations.

Alexei Leonov March 18, 1965

Spacecraft: Voskhod 2

Achievements:

  • First spacewalk (12 minutes outside the spacecraft).
  • Pioneered extravehicular activity (EVA).

Neil Armstrong July 20, 1969

Spacecraft: Apollo 11

Achievements:

  • First human to walk on the Moon.
  • Famous quote: "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind."
  • Collected lunar samples and conducted experiments.

Buzz Aldrin July 20, 1969

Spacecraft: Apollo 11

Achievements:

  • Second person to walk on the Moon.
  • Assisted in lunar sample collection and experiments.

Michael Collins July 20, 1969

Spacecraft: Apollo 11

Achievements:

  • Remained in lunar orbit during the Moon landing.
  • Played a critical role in mission success and communication.

Sally Ride June 18, 1983

Spacecraft: Challenger (STS-7)

Achievements:

  • First American woman in space.
  • Conducted experiments on microgravity and materials.
